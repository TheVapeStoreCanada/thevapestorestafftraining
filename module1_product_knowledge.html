Device types

Customers walk in knowing roughly what they want — or nothing at all. Understanding the device categories lets you meet them exactly where they are.

💨
Disposables
Pre-filled, pre-charged, no buttons. Use it and bin it. Great entry point for new switchers.
🔋
Pod systems
Rechargeable battery, swappable pods. Compact and low-maintenance. Huge seller.
⚙️
Refillable devices
Rechargeable battery, refillable tank or pod. Affordable upgrade from disposables for regular users.
🔧
Box mods
Adjustable wattage/temp, replaceable batteries. For experienced users who want full control.
Disposables
How they work & who buys them
›

Disposables are a sealed, all-in-one unit — battery, coil, and e-liquid in one body. When the battery or liquid runs out, the whole device is disposed of.

FeatureDetails
Puff countTypically 300–6,000+ puffs depending on model and price point
Nicotine typeAlmost always nic salts (smooth, fast-absorbing)
FlavoursPre-set — wide range available, fruity/menthol dominate
Price point$15–$65 depending on size and brand
Typical buyerPeople just quitting cigarettes, casual or social vapers, travellers
💡 Sales tip: Disposables are the easiest conversion for smokers. If someone walks in asking "what's easiest to use?" — start here. Then plant a seed about pods for cost savings long-term.
Pod systems
Closed vs open pod — know the difference
›

Pod systems come in two types. Knowing which is which prevents a lot of frustrated returns.

TypeHow it worksWho it's for
Closed podPre-filled pods you swap out. No filling required.Low maintenance users. Former smokers wanting simplicity.
Open podRefillable pod you fill yourself with bottled e-liquid.Regular vapers wanting variety and lower cost per puff.
💡Sales tip: Open pods have a higher upfront cost but lower ongoing cost. If a customer buys closed pods regularly, suggest transitioning to open pods — it saves them money and builds loyalty with your bottled juice selection.
Refillable devices
Types, components & maintenance
›

Refillable devices include vape pens, tube mods, and refillable AIOs (all-in-ones). They use a rechargeable battery, a refillable tank or pod, and replaceable coils. They're a great step-up device for someone who's been using disposables for a few months.

ComponentPurpose
Tank / clearomizerHolds the e-liquid. Usually 2–4ml capacity.
Coil / atomizerHeats the liquid to create vapour. Needs replacing every 1–3 weeks.
BatteryBuilt-in, rechargeable via USB-C (on modern pens).
Airflow ringControls draw tightness — tighter = more cigarette-like.
💡Sales tip: When selling a refillable device, always sell coils at the same time. A customer who runs out of coils and can't get back to buy more will switch brands. Suggest a 3-pack of coils as part of the bundle.
Box mods
Wattage, temperature control & batteries
›

Box mods are for the experienced vaper who wants total control. Unless a customer comes in asking specifically for one, don't lead with this category — it can overwhelm newer users and stall a sale.

FeatureWhat it means for the customer
Variable wattageAdjust power to get more or less vapour and flavour intensity
Temperature controlPrevent dry hits and fine-tune the experience
Replaceable batteries18650 cells — never need to replace the whole device, just the battery
Sub-ohm tanksUsed with box mods for high-vapour, direct-lung hits
⚠️Note: If a customer wants replaceable batteries, always ask if they have a battery charger. Selling loose lithium batteries without a safe charger is a liability and a poor experience.
E-liquids

E-liquid is often the highest-margin repeat purchase in the store. Staff who understand it well can guide customers to a bottle they'll come back for every week.

VG / PG ratios
What the numbers mean and when they matter
›

VG (Vegetable Glycerin) and PG (Propylene Glycol) are the two base liquids in every e-juice. Their ratio dramatically changes the vaping experience.

RatioVapourThroat hitFlavourBest for
50/50MediumNoticeableSharpPods, pens, MTL users
70/30 VGLarge cloudsSmoothGoodSub-ohm tanks, experienced users
Max VGHuge cloudsVery smoothMildCloud chasers, low-resistance builds
💡Quick rule: If someone uses a pod or a pen, steer them toward 50/50 or nic salt. If they have a big tank or mod, they likely want 70/30 VG. Always confirm before recommending.
⚠️High-VG juice in a pod system will clog coils quickly. A customer who runs back complaining about burnt taste may have the wrong VG/PG for their device.
Nicotine types
Freebase vs nic salts — the most important distinction
›
Freebase nicotineNicotine salts (nic salts)
FeelHarsher at higher strengthsSmooth even at high mg
AbsorptionSlower into bloodstreamFaster — closer to cigarettes
Common strengths3mg, 6mg, 12mg3mg, 6mg, 12mg, 20mg (20mg is the legal max in Canada)
Best deviceMods, sub-ohm tanksPods, disposables, pens
Best forExperienced vapers, cloud usersNew switchers from cigarettes
💡For new smokers switching: Always start with nic salts at 20mg (the Canadian maximum). The faster absorption satisfies cravings quicker, which reduces the urge to go back to cigarettes. As they get comfortable vaping, they can step down to 12mg over time.
Nicotine strength
Matching strength to smoking habits
›

Recommending the wrong nicotine strength is one of the most common reasons new vapers give up and go back to cigarettes. Get this right and you keep the customer.

Smoking habitRecommended nic salt strengthNotes
Heavy smoker (20+ cigs/day)20mgMaximum allowed in Canada — the right starting point for heavy smokers
Moderate (10–20 cigs/day)12mg–20mgMost common starting range — gauge by how satisfied they feel
Light (under 10 cigs/day)6mg–12mgLower end works well; freebase at 6mg is also an option
Already vapingMatch their current device/setupAsk what they're using and at what strength
Just want nicotine-free0mgAvailable in most juice lines — confirm before handing over
⚠️Canadian regulation: Health Canada caps nicotine concentration at 20mg/mL for all vaping products sold in Canada. Never recommend or sell anything above this limit.
Flavours
Flavour categories and how to guide customers
›

Flavour preference is personal but there are patterns. Learning to quickly identify what category a customer likes helps you narrow down from dozens of options to two or three confident suggestions.

CategoryExamplesCommon buyer profile
FruitMango, watermelon, strawberry, lycheeMost popular overall — safe default recommendation
Menthol / IceMint, glacier, iced berry, cool mangoFormer menthol smokers, people who like a sharp exhale
TobaccoVirginia tobacco, RY4, caramel tobaccoSmokers who want familiarity during the switch
Dessert / SweetCustard, cereal, candy, cheesecakeExperienced vapers who want all-day flavour
BeverageLemonade, cola, energy drink, coffeeAdventurous buyers, good for upselling second bottles
💡Conversion tip: New smokers switching often start with tobacco or menthol. Once they've made the switch and are comfortable, introduce them to fruit flavours — they almost always become their long-term preference, which increases flavour variety sales.
Key terminology

Customers use this language. Staff should too — it builds trust fast and prevents confusion. Learn these before your first shift on the floor.

Device & hardware terms

Coil / Atomizer
The replaceable heating element inside a tank or pod. Needs regular replacement to avoid burnt taste.
Ohm (Ω)
Measures coil resistance. Lower ohm = more power, more vapour. Sub-ohm means below 1.0Ω.
MTL (Mouth-to-Lung)
Drawing vapour into your mouth first, then inhaling — like smoking a cigarette. Most natural for new switchers.
DTL / DL (Direct Lung)
Inhaling vapour directly into the lungs, like a bong hit. More vapour, less throat hit. Common with mods.
Wattage
The power output of a device. Higher wattage = hotter coil = more vapour. Too high = burnt taste.
Priming
Letting a new coil soak in liquid before first use. Prevents burnt coils. Always tell customers this.
mAh
Battery capacity. Higher mAh = longer battery life between charges. Important for buyers asking "how long does it last?"
Dry hit
Vaping on an empty or unprimed coil — tastes burnt and harsh. Caused by low juice or coil not being primed.
Throat hit
The feeling at the back of the throat when inhaling. Higher nicotine and PG = stronger throat hit.
Cloud chasing
Prioritising large vapour clouds. Uses high-VG juice, low-resistance coils, and high-wattage mods.

E-liquid terms

PG (Propylene Glycol)
Thinner base liquid. Carries flavour well, provides throat hit. Used more in MTL/pod juices.
VG (Vegetable Glycerin)
Thicker, sweeter base. Produces large clouds. Dominant in sub-ohm juices.
Nic salts
Nicotine bound to an acid — absorbs faster, smoother at high mg. Standard in pods and disposables.
Freebase nicotine
Standard nicotine form — harsher at high strengths. Used in lower-mg juices for mods.
Steeping
Letting juice sit sealed to mature the flavour over time — like wine. Some customers ask about this.
Shortfill
A large bottle filled slightly below capacity, leaving room to add a nic shot to DIY the nicotine level.
💡 On the floor: You don't need to define these terms to every customer — but when a customer uses one, match their language. If they say "I'm having dry hits on my tank," don't say "sorry what?" — ask "what coil are you running and how full is your tank?" It builds immediate credibility.
