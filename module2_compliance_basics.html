Age & ID rules

The legal age to purchase vaping products in Ontario is 19. This is non-negotiable — and the rules around when and how to check ID are specific.

19
Minimum age to purchase in Ontario
Under the Smoke-Free Ontario Act, 2017, no one under 19 may purchase or be sold vaping products. This applies to every transaction — in-store, every time.
25
Ask for ID if the customer appears under 25
Ontario law requires retailers to request government-issued photo ID from anyone who appears younger than 25. When in doubt, card them. No exceptions for regulars.
ID check
Step-by-step: how to check an ID correctly
›
👤
Step 1 — Does the customer appear under 25?
If yes, you must ask for ID before completing the sale. If they clearly look well over 25, you may use your judgment — but when unsure, always ask. You will never get in trouble for asking.
🪪
Step 2 — Request government-issued photo ID
Acceptable forms: Ontario driver's licence, Ontario Photo Card, Canadian passport, Canadian permanent resident card, Canadian military ID. The ID must include a photo and date of birth. Store loyalty cards and student IDs are not acceptable. Health cards can be accepted if the customer offers one, but you must never ask for a health card as ID.
🔍
Step 3 — Inspect the ID carefully
Check: Does the photo match the person in front of you? Is the date of birth showing them to be 19 or older today? Does the ID look genuine — correct font, no warping, no peeling? Hold it up to the light if you're unsure about holograms.
✓
If ID is valid and they're 19+ — proceed with the sale
Complete the transaction normally. You don't need to record anything — just be satisfied they meet the age requirement.
✗
If ID is refused, looks fake, or they're under 19 — decline the sale
Say clearly and calmly: "I'm not able to complete this sale without valid ID showing you're 19 or older." Do not argue. Do not be pressured. Notify a manager if needed.
⚠️Never request a health card as ID. Ontario law prohibits asking for a health card. However, if a customer voluntarily presents one alongside confirming their DOB, you may accept it. A gym membership card or a piece of paper is never acceptable under any circumstances. Government-issued photo ID with date of birth remains the standard.

Fake ID
Spotting altered or fake ID — what to look for
›

You are not expected to be a forensic document specialist. But there are common signs that should make you pause.

What to checkRed flag
PhotoPhoto doesn't match the person, looks like it's been replaced, or is a different print quality from the rest of the card
Date of birthNumbers look a different font, colour, or size from the rest of the text — possible alteration
Card materialFeels flimsy, peeling, or has an unusual texture compared to a real Ontario licence
HologramsOntario licences have security holograms — if you tilt it and see nothing, it may be fake
Magnetic stripe / chipMissing or visibly damaged on a card that should have one
BehaviourCustomer is nervous, avoids eye contact, hands over the ID upside down or covered
⚠️If you suspect fake ID: Decline the sale politely. Say "I'm not able to accept this ID." You are not required to confiscate it or confront the customer. Call a manager. You cannot be penalised for refusing a sale — only for completing one you shouldn't have.
Penalties
What happens if a sale to a minor occurs
›

Ontario and the federal government both have enforcement mechanisms. Undercover compliance checks — where officials send someone underage to attempt a purchase — are actively used in Ontario.

Who is penalisedConsequence
The individual staff memberPersonal fine issued to the employee who made the sale
The store owner / businessFines up to $100,000 for repeat violations
The store licenceSuspension or permanent revocation of the right to sell vaping products
⚠️You are personally liable. If you sell to a minor, the fine can be issued to you individually — not just the store. "They looked old enough" is not a legal defence if the person appeared under 25 and you didn't ask for ID.
ℹ️Compliance checks: Public health inspectors may send someone who appears under 25 into the store to attempt a purchase. This is standard practice in Ontario. Always follow the process — every single time.
Flavour & product rules

As a registered specialty vape store, The Vape Store operates under different rules than convenience stores. Understanding what you can sell — and to whom — matters every day on the floor.

Specialty vape stores
What a specialty vape store is allowed to sell
›

Under Ontario's Smoke-Free Ontario Act, 2017 (as updated July 2020), vaping product sales are restricted based on store type. Specialty vape stores — which The Vape Store qualifies as — have expanded permissions that general retailers like convenience stores do not have.

Product typeGeneral retailer (e.g. corner store)Specialty vape store (The Vape Store)
Tobacco, mint & menthol flavours✓ Allowed✓ Allowed
All other flavours (fruit, dessert, etc.)✗ Prohibited✓ Allowed
Products up to 20mg/mL nicotine✓ Allowed✓ Allowed
Products over 20mg/mL nicotine✗ Prohibited✗ Prohibited (20mg is the federal cap)
ℹ️To maintain specialty vape store status, at least 85% of the store's total sales or inventory purchases must consist of vaping products. This is managed at ownership level — but it's useful to understand why the store's product mix matters.
Nicotine cap
20mg/mL — Canada's federal nicotine limit
›

Health Canada sets a maximum nicotine concentration of 20mg per millilitre for all vaping products sold in Canada. This is a federal rule that applies everywhere — no province can go higher.

⚠️If a customer asks for "50mg nic salts" or "35mg juice" — these are not legal in Canada. Do not order them, do not sell them, and do not tell a customer they can find them locally. Politely explain that 20mg is the maximum available in Canada and recommend the best 20mg option for their needs.
💡Most customers switching from cigarettes who ask for higher nicotine are used to products from the US market. Reassure them that 20mg nic salts in Canada are specifically formulated to absorb quickly and satisfy cravings — they work well for heavy smokers.
Excise stamps
What is an excise stamp and why it matters
›

Since 2022, all vaping e-liquid and pre-filled pods sold in Canada must carry a federal excise duty stamp. Ontario participates in the coordinated excise stamp program.

What you need to knowDetails
What it looks likeA small stamp or mark on the product packaging — typically on the bottle or pod packaging label
What it meansThe excise duty has been paid and the product came through a licensed Canadian importer or distributor
If a product has no stampDo not sell it. Flag it to a manager immediately. Selling unstamped goods is a federal offence.
Who is responsibleThe store must only carry excise-stamped stock. If a product arrives without a stamp, do not put it on the shelf.
⚠️Unstamped products are illegal to sell in Canada. If a customer brings in an unmarked bottle or device they purchased elsewhere and asks you to identify it, that is not your product — you are not responsible. But you cannot sell it or process it as a return for store credit.
Advertising
What you can and cannot say or display
›

The federal TVPA places strict limits on how vaping products can be advertised and promoted — including inside stores and on social media.

PermittedProhibited

ℹ️As a specialty vape store, in-store displays are permitted — but they must not be visible from outside. This means window displays featuring vaping products are not allowed. This is enforced by inspectors.
Where & how you sell

Compliance isn't only about what you sell — it's about where vaping is allowed, what signage must be posted, and how to handle difficult situations on the floor.

No vaping zones
Where vaping is banned in Ontario
›

Under the Smoke-Free Ontario Act, 2017, vaping is prohibited anywhere smoking is prohibited. This matters both for customers sampling in-store and for staff.

LocationRule
Enclosed workplaces and public spacesVaping banned — this includes bars, restaurants, malls, offices
Within 9 metres of hospital entrancesNo vaping in this buffer zone
School grounds and playgroundsNo vaping on property
Within 20 metres of school propertyStricter enforcement as of late 2025
Vehicles with anyone under 16Vaping in a vehicle with a minor under 16 is illegal
Sports fields and recreational facilitiesNo vaping during use
ℹ️In-store sampling: As a specialty vape store, limited sampling is permitted under specific conditions — maximum two people at one time, one-time disposable mouthpieces unless the customer uses their own device. No cannabis, tobacco, or controlled substances in the device being sampled. Confirm current store policy with your manager before offering samples.

Scenario practice

Read each scenario and the correct response. These are common real-world situations every staff member will face.

High risk
"I forgot my ID at home but I promise I'm 19. I come in here all the time, you know me."
Correct response
"I completely understand and I'm sorry for the hassle. Unfortunately I have to check ID for anyone who looks under 25 — it's the law and I can't make exceptions even for regulars. If you can bring ID next time, we'll get you sorted."
Judgment call
A customer hands you an Ontario health card as their only ID.
Correct response
"Thanks — just so you know, I can't ask you for your health card as ID, but since you've offered it I can accept it. Health cards don't show your date of birth, so I'll need you to confirm that for me — what's your date of birth?" Once confirmed, proceed if they're 19+.
Judgment call
A customer presents an out-of-province driver's licence from British Columbia. They clearly look around 20.
Correct response
Canadian provincial driver's licences from other provinces are generally acceptable. Check the photo, the DOB (ensure they are 19+), and that it's not expired. If it passes all checks, proceed. If anything seems off, ask for a second piece of ID or call your manager.
Judgment call
A customer asks if you have "50mg nic salts" because they order them from the US.
Correct response
"That strength isn't legal to sell in Canada — Health Canada caps nicotine at 20mg/mL nationwide. What I can tell you is our 20mg nic salts are formulated to absorb really well — most people switching from cigarettes find they do the job. Want me to show you what we have in 20mg?"
High risk
A customer becomes aggressive when you ask for ID, demanding to speak to a manager and saying you're discriminating against them.
Correct response
"I absolutely understand your frustration, and I'm happy to get my manager. I do need to clarify — checking ID for anyone who appears under 25 is a legal requirement under Ontario law, not a personal decision. It applies to every customer equally. I'll get my manager for you right now."
